Project Overview

Modernising India's
most trusted bank
for the digital era.

The goal was to modernise the HDFC Bank website experience while maintaining trust, scale, and regulatory clarity. The redesign focused on simplifying navigation, reducing cognitive load, and increasing digital product conversions across all key customer journeys.

PROBLEM

Problem Statement

The legacy experience was
working against the user.

The legacy HDFC Bank website suffered from high information density, fragmented navigation, limited accessibility, and outdated UI patterns. Key customer actions like logging into NetBanking, comparing products, or applying online required excessive effort and time — directly impacting conversion and trust.

01
Dense Multi-Level Navigation
Users couldn't find products quickly. Navigation had 4–5 nested levels causing high drop-off at the discovery stage.
02
Cognitive Overload
Overloaded banners, heavy financial jargon and competing CTAs created decision paralysis across every page.
03
Accessibility Gaps
Limited keyboard support, poor colour contrast, and screen-reader incompatibilities excluded a large segment of users.
04
Indirect Conversion Flows
Critical journeys — loan applications, credit card sign-ups, NetBanking — had too many friction points and unnecessary redirects.

Research & Discovery

Understanding the
real friction
through data.

A multi-method research approach combining qualitative and quantitative signals to build a precise picture of where — and why — users were abandoning the experience.

i. Stakeholder interviews across marketing, product, and customer support teams to align on business objectives
ii. Full UX audit of the legacy live site — heuristic evaluation, task analysis, and session recordings
iii. Funnel analysis using SimilarWeb-style engagement metrics to identify highest drop-off pages
iv. Competitive benchmarking across leading Indian banks (SBI, ICICI, Kotak) and global digital-first banks

UX Strategy

Five pillars.
One clear
direction.

01
Simplified Information Architecture
Restructured site taxonomy from 5 levels to 3 — grounded in card-sorting sessions with real users, not internal org charts.
02
Primary Task-Based Navigation
Navigation rebuilt around user intent ("I want to…") rather than product categories, reducing time-to-task by an estimated 40%.
03
Strong Visual Hierarchy for CTAs
Clear primary, secondary, and tertiary CTA hierarchy across every page — eliminating competing calls to action and decision paralysis.
04
Content Simplification for Financial Literacy
Financial jargon replaced with plain-language content — increasing comprehension and reducing calls to customer support.
05
WCAG-Aligned Accessibility
Full AA compliance: colour contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, ARIA labels, and screen-reader testing built into every component.

Outcome & Learnings

Design that
earns trust
at scale.

This case study demonstrates how thoughtful UX design in regulated industries like banking can drive measurable business outcomes. By aligning user needs with business objectives, the HDFC Bank website now delivers clarity, confidence, and conversion at scale — across 160M+ monthly touchpoints.

◈
Trust is a design decision
In banking, visual clarity and accessibility aren't just good UX — they directly build institutional trust and reduce anxiety around financial decisions.
◈
Simplification is hard work
Reducing cognitive load required more iterations than adding features. Every cut had to be defended with user data and stakeholder alignment.
◈
Scalability from day one
A robust design system meant the bank could scale new product pages and campaigns without breaking the experience — compounding design value over time.
◈
Accessibility is not a constraint
WCAG compliance opened the site to millions of users who were previously excluded — and improved the experience for everyone.
