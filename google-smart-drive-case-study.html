Project Overview

Tracking every
kilometre mapped
for the world.

Google Street View is built on billions of panoramic images captured by drivers operating specialised camera cars across the globe. Smart Drive is the mobile app designed to track these drivers' daily field activity — scoring performance, guiding them through scan points, flagging weather and traffic incidents, and replacing the fragmented paper-form process that preceded it.

Problem Statement

A global mapping operation
running on paper forms
and manual logs.

Street View drivers across 50 countries were onboarding, logging daily work, and reporting incidents through Google Forms — a paper-equivalent process with no real-time intelligence, no performance visibility, and no way to support drivers during accidents, weather changes, or route diversions. There were no comparable apps to benchmark against, and the interconnected nature of the flows made every edge case a unique design challenge.

01
Paper-Based Onboarding & Activity Logging
Drivers recorded daily work — odometer readings, scan point completions, shift start/end — on Google Forms. No automation, no real-time sync, no performance feedback. Time logs had no digital baseline to measure against.
02
No Real-Time Driver Support in the Field
When accidents, weather changes, or road incidents occurred, drivers had no intelligent support layer. There was no app to push warnings, suggest route diversions, or flag location-based risks mid-shift.
03
No Performance Visibility or Incentive System
Without scoring, high-performing drivers received no recognition and attrition remained high. There was no mechanism to reward work completion, identify top performers, or build accountability across a 50-country operation.
04
No Competitive Benchmarking Possible
No similar app existed for Street View driver operations — making conventional design benchmarking impossible. Every UX decision had to be built from first principles, grounded in behaviour research rather than existing patterns.

Research & Discovery

Understanding the
field driver
from the ground up.

With no comparable app to benchmark against, the design had to be built entirely from behavioural research. We mapped a detailed archetype of the Street View driver to establish the right UX baseline across 50 countries and languages.

i.Google Forms proficiency — drivers were comfortable with basic digital data entry for reporting purposes, giving us a low-end baseline for digital literacy to design from
ii.Proficiency in local language, limited English — with 50+ countries in scope, every symbol, icon, and label had to be globally intuitive and culturally tested to avoid misinterpretation
iii.Not digital natives — users were comfortable with mobile for WhatsApp, YouTube, and photography, but not experienced with complex app navigation or multi-step digital workflows
iv.Field testing on real driver dashboards — the app runs on a standee on the car dashboard while driving, requiring ergonomic validation of font sizes, button tap targets, and contrast ratios in direct sunlight

UX Strategy

Safety.
Ease of Use.
Automation.

01
Clear Symbols & Global Icons
Every icon is globally intuitive and closely associated with its intended meaning. With 50+ countries in scope, cultural differences were actively mapped — symbols that could be misinterpreted in certain regions were replaced with universally safe alternatives.
02
Progressive Disclosure
Step-by-step workflows with smart defaults eliminate information overload. Drivers focus on the immediate task — what to do right now — rather than navigating complex menus. This minimises errors and increases speed across non-digital-native users.
03
Colour & Contrast for Safety
The app uses a black background to support both day and night driving. A minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 between background and icons or text — aligned with Google's Automotive OS design guidelines — ensures readability on a dashboard standee at all light conditions.
04
Voice Enablement
Voice confirmation and "Say it aloud" prompts are embedded across critical interaction points — ensuring drivers can confirm actions eyes-free while driving, upholding safety as the non-negotiable guiding principle throughout the experience.
05
Smart Plugin Integrations
Weather API warnings and predictions surface automatically. Google Maps integration pushes traffic alerts and route diversions. Location tracking detects accidents and rest-area pauses — automating intelligence that drivers previously had no access to in the field.

Outcome & Learnings

Design without
benchmarks
starts with behaviour.

There was no comparable app for Street View driver operations — so conventional benchmarking was impossible. Every design decision had to be grounded in field research and behavioural archetypes rather than competitive references. That constraint became the project's biggest design advantage: we built from real-world truth, not industry convention.

◈
Behaviour archetype over persona assumptions
We spent more time profiling the archetype — digital literacy, language constraints, mobile habits, physical context — than we did on traditional personas. This grounding informed every icon choice, tap target, and language decision across 50+ countries.
◈
Ergonomics are a safety feature, not an afterthought
Field testing on a real dashboard standee in sunlight exposed contrast failures, font size issues, and button tap target problems that no Figma preview would have caught. Physical context testing became a non-negotiable step in the design process.
◈
No benchmarking requires stronger first principles
Without competitor apps to reference, we had to derive every UX decision from first principles — flow logic, progressive disclosure depth, icon universality. It was harder and slower, but produced a more original and context-fit design.
◈
Reward-driven performance scales globally
Score visibility, shift badges, and leaderboard ranking turned daily activity logging from a chore into a competitive habit — reducing attrition across the driver pool and contributing directly to the 1.8M ROI outcome.
