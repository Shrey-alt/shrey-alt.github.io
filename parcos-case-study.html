Project Overview

Bringing India's
luxury beauty world
fully online.

Parcos is India's premier multi-brand luxury beauty retailer — home to 300+ international brands across fragrance, makeup, skin and hair. The challenge was to transform a legacy e-commerce experience into a truly premium digital destination that reflects the elegance of the brands it carries, while driving measurable growth in a fiercely competitive market.

Problem Statement

A premium brand
trapped in a generic digital experience.

Parcos carries Gucci, Kenzo, Dior, YSL, and 300+ other luxury labels — yet its digital experience felt no different from a mass-market beauty retailer. The previous platform was cluttered, navigationally inconsistent, and completely failed to communicate the editorial authority and brand prestige that Parcos holds in offline retail. It was losing ground to Nykaa Luxe, Sephora.in, and international direct-to-consumer sites.

01
No Luxury Positioning Online
The digital experience felt generic — product-dump grids with no editorial narrative. The in-store luxury feel was completely lost online, undermining brand trust at the highest-intent moment.
02
Fragmented Brand Discovery
Users couldn't navigate 300+ luxury brands intuitively. No coherent brand universe, no filtering by prestige tier, no way to explore brand stories — just an alphabetical dump.
03
Checkout Drop-Off on Mobile
High-AOV (average order value) luxury purchases require trust at every step. The mobile checkout had friction points and lacked the reassurance cues that premium buyers expect before committing ₹10,000+ orders.
04
No Editorial / Content Layer
Luxury e-commerce isn't just transactions — it's aspiration. Competitors were building strong content ecosystems. Parcos had no editorial hub, no beauty journalism, no gifting guides to earn top-of-funnel trust.

Research & Discovery

Understanding
the luxury beauty
buyer in India.

A 10-week discovery sprint that combined behavioural analytics from the existing platform with qualitative interviews, competitive teardowns, and luxury retail benchmarking across global markets.

i.In-depth interviews with 30 luxury beauty shoppers across Tier 1 cities — segmented by spend level, brand loyalty, and channel preference (in-store vs. online)
ii.Heatmap and funnel analysis across the existing platform — identifying the top 6 drop-off pages and session abandonment triggers on mobile
iii.Competitive teardowns of Sephora.com, Nykaa Luxe, Cult Beauty, Space NK, and Net-a-Porter's beauty vertical — benchmarking editorial, navigation, and checkout UX
iv.Brand partnership workshops with Parcos' key luxury brand partners to understand co-branding and brand-experience requirements within the platform

UX Strategy

Editorial
first. Commerce
always.

01
Editorial-First Homepage Architecture
Rebuilt the homepage as a luxury magazine cover — rotating hero campaigns, brand-of-the-week features, and seasonal editorial drops before any product grid. Aspiration first, conversion second.
02
Cohesive Brand Universe Navigation
Redesigned the Brands section as a curated brand directory — searchable, filterable by category and prestige tier, with individual brand pages that tell each brand's story and hero products.
03
Gifting as a First-Class Journey
Built a dedicated Gifting experience with occasion-based curation (Birthday, Anniversary, Diwali), price filters, and curated gift sets — reducing gifting decision time from 20+ minutes to under 6.
04
Luxezine — The Editorial Hub
Designed Luxezine as an in-platform content layer: beauty journalism, brand stories, fragrance guides, and trend reports. Positioned Parcos as India's luxury beauty authority, not just a retailer.
05
Frictionless High-Trust Checkout
Overhauled the mobile checkout with luxury trust signals — gift wrapping options, personal notes, white-glove delivery, and gift receipt — tailored to the emotional stakes of premium purchases.

Outcome & Learnings

Luxury UX
is emotion
before conversion.

The defining insight of this project: luxury buyers don't just want to shop — they want to feel. The most impactful design decisions weren't about reducing friction (though we did that too), but about adding the right friction — the pause, the reveal, the story — that makes a ₹12,000 fragrance feel worth every rupee before it's even in the cart.

◈
Editorial and commerce are not opponents
Luxezine drove 28% of new user acquisition and increased average basket size by 18% — proving that content investment in luxury directly converts. The users who read, buy more.
◈
The gift is a design problem
Gifting UX demanded as much care as the core purchase flow. When we solved the "what to buy, how to present it, how to personalise it" experience, gifting AOV exceeded product AOV by 2.3×.
◈
Brand partners need their own stage
Giving each luxury brand its own curated page — with imagery, story, and hero products — increased brand page time-on-site by 4× and improved brand partner satisfaction, opening doors to co-marketing.
◈
Trust is the highest-converting design element
Every trust signal we added to mobile checkout — authenticity guarantees, white-glove delivery, gift wrapping, personal notes — directly reduced cart abandonment for orders above ₹8,000.
