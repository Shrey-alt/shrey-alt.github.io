Project Overview

Propelling Parcos
E-Commerce
onto a Growth Trajectory.

Parcos — "The Beauty of Luxury" — is India's premier multi-brand luxury beauty retailer. The brief had three clear objectives: design engagement opportunities through richer marketing and distribution touchpoints, build a seamless buying and browsing experience, and portray an authentic brand identity that does justice to the 300+ luxury houses on the platform.

Problem Statement

A premium brand
trapped in a generic digital experience.

Parcos carries Gucci, Kenzo, Dior, YSL, and 300+ other luxury labels — yet its digital experience felt no different from a mass-market beauty retailer. Sessions averaged just 1m 3s, the bounce rate sat at 72.63%, and conversions languished at 0.46%. The platform had the right brands but none of the experience those brands deserve.

01
No Sensory Bridge Online
81% of focus-group participants wanted a way to experience a fragrance's scent profile digitally before buying — a fundamental gap the existing platform didn't address at all.
02
Authenticity Anxiety
80% of shoppers didn't know where products came from or how to verify their authenticity. Without credibility signals, high-AOV luxury purchases were a trust gamble — driving users to brand DTC sites instead.
03
Discovery Paralysis
59% couldn't narrow down fragrances across 300+ options. No scent-family guidance, no Perfume Finder, no intelligent filtering — the catalogue was overwhelming rather than inviting.
04
Discount-First Positioning
60% said discounts drove their online purchase decisions — a pattern that eroded the luxury perception Parcos had built offline and positioned it as a promotions channel rather than a premium destination.

Research & Discovery

Design for
humans — not
catalogues.

A structured focus-group study with luxury beauty shoppers uncovered the real barriers to online luxury buying — emotional, functional, and trust-based. The insights directly shaped every strategic design decision.

81%of shoppers wanted to experience a fragrance's scent online before purchasing — a sensory gap that the Perfume Finder was built to bridge
80%didn't know product origin or how to verify authenticity — driving the Certificate of Authenticity and transparent sourcing features
63%of male shoppers impulse-buy fragrances — informing editorial-led discovery and curated "Shop the Mood" homepage modules
59%said they couldn't narrow down fragrances on their own — the core brief for the Perfume Finder segmentation tool
52%follow e-commerce aggregators rather than brand DTC sites — validating Parcos' platform strategy and need for influencer pages
51%use scent families (floral, woody, oriental) as mood guides — the UX taxonomy that underpins all fragrance filtering and PDP design

UX Strategy

Editorial
first. Commerce
always.

01
Perfume Finder — Encourage Trust & Confident Buying
The centrepiece innovation: a scent-segmentation tool that walks users through fragrance notes and mood families to narrow 300+ options to a personalised shortlist. Directly addresses the 59% who couldn't narrow fragrances and 81% who wanted a sensory experience digitally.
02
Immersive Product Detail Pages
Multiple PDP layout variations — tested via Attention Insight heatmaps — designed to convey sensory richness: scent family maps, ingredient storytelling, fragrance pyramid visualisation, and size/concentration selectors built for confident high-AOV decisions.
03
Interactive Brand Pages
Each of the 300+ luxury brands gets its own curated editorial page — brand story, hero products, and campaign imagery. Designed to turn discoverability into brand affinity and give Parcos' brand partners a premium digital stage.
04
Certificate of Authenticity
A dedicated trust layer addressing the 80% authenticity concern from research — every luxury product links to a verifiable origin certificate, turning the biggest online luxury barrier into a Parcos-exclusive advantage.
05
Personalised Gift Wrap + Influencer Pages
Gifting elevated to a first-class journey with personalised wrapping options and handwritten notes. Influencer pages curate trusted "shelfies" and scent diaries, bringing social discovery natively into the platform and capturing the 52% who follow aggregators.

Outcome & Learnings

Luxury UX
is trust
before conversion.

The defining insight: 80% of users didn't trust product authenticity, and 81% couldn't evaluate a fragrance without a sensory bridge. Every percentage point of that 501% conversion uplift traces back to solving those two problems — not through visual polish, but through genuine functional empathy with how luxury buyers make decisions.

◈
Solve the sensory gap — or lose the sale
81% of users wanted to experience a scent online. The Perfume Finder wasn't a nice-to-have — it was the single most confidence-building feature on the platform, and the primary driver of the session duration jump from 1m 3s to 4m 49s.
◈
Authenticity is the luxury trust signal
Addressing the 80% authenticity concern with Certificate of Authenticity and transparent sourcing had a direct, measurable impact on conversion — particularly for orders above ₹5,000 on mobile, where the trust barrier is highest.
◈
Test before you commit — heatmaps don't lie
Running 5–8 product card variations and multiple PDP layouts through Attention Insight heatmap testing before finalising saved costly late-stage design pivots. The data consistently favoured scent-hierarchy layouts over price-first grids.
◈
Mobile-first is not optional at 88%
With 88% of all Parcos traffic on mobile, every design decision had to pass the mobile-first test. Lifting mobile conversion from 0.40% to 2.54% was a direct result of treating mobile as the primary experience — not an afterthought.
