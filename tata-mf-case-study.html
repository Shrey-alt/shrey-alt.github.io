Project Overview

Building India's
smartest mutual fund

Tata Mutual Fund had a robust desktop web platform — but no dedicated mobile app. With 80%+ of Indian investors primarily on mobile, the brief was clear: design a zero-to-one mobile experience that moves beyond a desktop-to-mobile port and delivers a genuinely native, intelligent investing journey.

Problem Statement

A desktop-first product
failing a mobile-first generation.

Tata Mutual Fund's existing web platform was built for desktop — dense data tables, multi-step forms, and complex navigation that simply broke on mobile. Meanwhile, first-time investors were choosing app-native competitors like Zerodha, Groww and ET Money, whose mobile experiences were built from first principles. Tata needed more than a responsive port. It needed a rethink.

01
No Native Mobile Presence
Users were forced onto a mobile-web experience that was never designed for thumb navigation, small screens, or on-the-go investing behaviour.
02
Complex Desktop-Ported Flows
SIP setup, KYC, and portfolio views were directly ported from desktop — requiring 8–12 steps for actions that should take 3 on mobile.
03
First-Timer Anxiety
New investors faced raw NAV tables and fund fact sheets with no guidance — creating high anxiety and abandonment before the first SIP.
04
Losing Ground to App-Native Rivals
Groww and Zerodha were onboarding first-time investors in under 5 minutes on mobile. Tata's web-first approach was visibly losing acquisition share.

Research & Discovery

Understanding
the mobile investor
from the ground up.

We ran a 6-week discovery sprint combining behavioural data from the existing web platform with primary research targeting first-time and lapsed mutual fund investors.

i.In-depth interviews with 24 mobile-first investors aged 22–38 — segmented by investing experience, income, and digital comfort
ii.Heatmap and session analysis of the existing mobile-web experience — identifying top 5 drop-off screens and friction hotspots
iii.Competitive teardowns of Groww, Zerodha Coin, ET Money, Paytm Money and Kuvera — benchmarking onboarding, SIP flow, and portfolio UX
iv.Jobs-to-be-Done workshops with internal PMs and distribution partners to align on the app's core value proposition and priority features

UX Strategy

Native by
design. Not a
port.

01
Mobile-First Information Architecture
Rebuilt the IA from scratch for thumb-reachable navigation — five bottom-tab zones replacing the desktop's top-nav mega-menu. Every screen designed for one hand.
02
Portfolio 360° — Industry First
Single-screen view of mutual funds, stocks, and linked bank accounts. Reduced context-switching across apps for users managing multiple asset classes.
03
Smart Onboarding in Under 4 Minutes
KYC, bank linking and first SIP compressed into a guided 3-step flow with progress indicators, inline validation and no unnecessary redirects.
04
Goal-Based Investing UX
FIRE Calculator, Retirement Planner and Financial Roadmap built into the core experience — shifting the mental model from "picking a fund" to "building a future."
05
Scalable Design System
Component library spanning 120+ components, covering both iOS and Android variants — enabling the engineering team to ship new features without design debt.

Outcome & Learnings

Mobile is a
different medium,
not a smaller screen.

The biggest learning was that porting a desktop experience to mobile — even a well-designed one — is never the answer. The Tata MF app worked because it was conceived mobile-first from the first whiteboard session, respecting thumb ergonomics, attention spans and the emotional context of making financial decisions on a phone.

◈
Investing confidence is designed, not assumed
First-time investors needed reassurance at every step — risk labels, plain-language descriptions, and real-time projections turned anxiety into commitment.
◈
Simplicity requires more iterations, not fewer
Compressing the KYC-to-SIP flow from 12 steps to 3 required 11 rounds of iteration. Removing friction is harder than adding features.
◈
A design system compounds its value over time
The 120+ component library meant the team could ship new fund categories and seasonal campaigns without touching the design team — true design leverage.
◈
Goal framing beats product framing
Users who entered through a goal (e.g. "Child's Education") had 2× higher SIP completion rates than those who browsed funds directly — validating the Financial Roadmap bet.
